How psychotherapy, or talking therapy works

How we will start

At your first appointment, you will be invited into a private and comfortable space to discuss your personal hopes in therapy. This initial consultation will provide time to discuss any concerns you may have about therapy.

If you think about it carefully, isn’t the relationship with your doctor, your dentist, your hair dresser, crucial for tending to what’s needed for your body, teeth or hair? Similarly, taking the time to find a good therapist for you is just as important. It is for this reason, I offer a free consultation, you can come in person or have a phone/video call to talk about anything regarding therapy and understand how I work as a psychotherapist.

I offer in-person sessions, and video sessions via Zoom, Google Meet, WhatsApp, and FaceTime for those who are not able to can’t come in person. Together, we will also explore the type of psychotherapy that will be best for your needs. You may have a preference for the kind of personal therapist you would like to see and if you feel I may not be the right therapist to work with, I would be happy to enable a referral, when appropriate.

Endings

How to end therapy is crucial for being in therapy. It is like anything else we may need to reflect on, how to end relationships, friendships or breakups, how to resign from a job, or how to tell someone you love things that hurt you – they’re all about ending status quo and making a change.

As I have said repeatedly, existential therapy is about autonomy, will and choice. Hence, it will be up to you when and how you may want to end therapy. I request and remind my clients to enable two sessions to reflective on this decision and enable it with certainty.
